## Our Approach

9th Caulfield Scout Group is run by members of our community. We warmly welcome new
members — young people, parents, and volunteers alike. If you're new to Scouting,
the [Scouts Victoria website](https://scoutsvictoria.com.au) is a great place to
learn more about the program.

Our committee meets monthly and includes community members, Scout leaders, and parents.
Everyone is welcome to come along.

## Our History

### How it started

The Troop was the first section to be formed, and in 1951 — before a hall had been
secured — meetings were held in the Smart's garage and at Kangaroo Park. Before long,
the group moved to the old Lauriston Tennis Club, then to the Murrumbeena Baptist
Church Hall. The Pack met at St Patrick's School Hall, and some meetings were even held
in the Sealy's home.

### Growing into our own hall

Scouting was booming after the Second World War, and families moving into Murrumbeena
brought a surge of new members. The Scout Group acquired a long-term lease on railway
land in the disused Linear Railway Reserve, near Hughesdale Station. Parents and the
local community joined forces to fund and build a proper hall, and in **1961** the
9th Caulfield (Murrumbeena) Scout Group had a home they could call their own.
